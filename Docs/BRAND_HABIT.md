## Copywriting Angles (Active)

### The "Environment" Angle (PRIMARY)
- Environment shapes behavior. The device physically modifies your space.
- A cue you can't swipe away. It stares at you.
- *"Make your good habits inevitable."*

### The "Simple Idea, Unexpected Results" Angle
- A light that turns on when you do the thing. That's it.
- *"Such a simple idea — but it works on you in ways you don't expect."*
- Viktor's explicit framing: NOT "stupidly simple" — "simple idea, unexpected results"

### The "Gift" Angle
- It has someone's name on it. Made by hand, with thought about a specific person.
- *"A device with their name on it, sitting on their desk, quietly working."*

### The "No Apps, No Guilt" Angle
- No notifications, no streak penalties, no app required
- *"The only tracker that doesn't judge you."*
- It just quietly displays the truth

---

## Voice: Always
- Write like Viktor is speaking. First person, honest, slightly informal.
- Be specific. "Twice as much exercise" beats "significant improvement."
- Acknowledge imperfection — this is Batch 1, handmade, say so.
- Short sentences. One idea per paragraph. Let silence work.
- Earn the claim before making it.

## Voice: Never
- "Transform your life." — Too big, not earned.
- "The only habit tracker you'll ever need." — Can't make this claim.
- "Proven by science." — Say which science, or don't say it.
- Fake urgency.
- Exclamation marks.
- "Stupidly simple" — Viktor explicitly rejected this.

---

## What Viktor Explicitly Rejected
- "Stupidly simple" — use "simple idea, unexpected results" instead
- "I couldn't stick to anything" — too sensational, not accurate
- "This wasn't supposed to work. It did." — not true
- Hero feeling = **curiosity**, NOT inspiration or urgency
- "Founders Edition" label on device
