# Competitive Landscape & Inspiration

*Last updated: March 2026. Updated to reflect AddOne (not Notch) positioning.*

---

## 1. The "Gold Standard" (Electronic)
### The Every Day Calendar (Simone Giertz)
- **Concept:** PCB with 365 touch-capacitive LEDs. Light up the day when you do the thing.
- **Kickstarter:** Raised $593,352 from 2,305 backers.
- **Price:** ~$300–$400 shipped.
- **Vibe:** Engineer-chic, raw PCB aesthetic, celebrity founder.
- **Why it worked:** Simone's personality + visual impact of a full year at a glance + tactile gold star mechanism.
- **Our position vs. this:** Hers tracks a year (365 days). AddOne tracks 21 weeks — a more achievable, milestone-based frame. Hers is a raw PCB. Ours is made to order with your name on it. We win on the personal/gift angle and the 21-week completion moment.

---

## 2. The "Analog" Premium
### Ugmonk Analog
- **Concept:** Wooden card holder + paper index cards. "The Simplest Productivity System."
- **Price:** $99 for a wooden block and paper.
- **Vibe:** Dieter Rams, minimalistic, slow productivity.
- **Why it works:** Anti-digital sentiment + physical ritual + desk jewelry.
- **Takeaway:** People pay $100 for a block of wood if the *story* is about "focus" and "craft."
- **Customer complaint:** "Sucked into buying a recurring subscription for new cards." — AddOne has no subscription, no refills, no recurring costs. One purchase.
- **Our position vs. this:** Paper doesn't glow. We add the feedback loop (the dopamine hit of the LED turning on). Same desk-jewelry aesthetic, active reward.

---

## 3. The "Interactive Art" Tier
### Love Hultén
- **Concept:** Custom synthesizers and consoles made of walnut, brass, and pastel keys.
- **Price:** $2,000–$4,000+
- **Vibe:** Retro-futurism, museum quality.
- **Takeaway:** The device must look like it belongs on an architect's desk, not a shelf at Best Buy.
- **Our position vs. this:** $149 is accessible. Same obsessive craft ethos at 10× less cost.

---

## 4. The "Quantified Self" Tools
### Timeular
- **Concept:** 8-sided Bluetooth die. Flip it to track time.
- **Price:** $69 + subscription.
- **Weakness:** Feels like a tool, not art. Requires app subscription (friction).
- **Our edge:** No subscription. No app required for daily use. The device IS the display.

---

## 5. The Habit App Market (The Real Competitor)
Apps like Streaks, Habitica, Apple Health, Notion habit templates.
- **The core problem:** When the phone is off, the habit doesn't exist. "Out of sight, out of mind."
- **The guilt loop:** Break a streak → shame → delete the app. Common pattern.
- **User language:** "Most habit apps are little more than a daily to-do list that attempt to guilt you into continuing."
- **Our edge:** The device is always visible. It can't be swiped away, closed, or silenced. It stares at you.

## Key Market Insight
There is a gap between **$20 journals/apps** and **$300+ electronic calendars**.

**AddOne at $149** sits in the "premium gift / accessible luxury" slot. It's the "Eames Chair" of habit trackers — not the cheapest, not the most expensive, but the one that feels considered.

The gift market is especially strong: something with someone's name on it, made by hand, with a real philosophy behind it. That's a different category than "productivity gadget."
